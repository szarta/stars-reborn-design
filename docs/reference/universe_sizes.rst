Universe Sizes Reference
========================

Source: `Stars! wiki — New Game Advanced Setup Step 1 <http://wiki.starsautohost.org/wiki/New_Game(Advanced_Setup)_Step_1:_Specify_the_Universe>`_

Universe Sizes
--------------

**Tiny** — 400 light years in size. 2–3 player game; players will encounter
each other early.

**Small** — 800 light years. Up to 4 players.

**Medium** — 1200 light years. Large for 4, up to 16. Early contact likely with
more than 12 players.

**Large** — 1600 light years. Easy for 16. 4 or fewer players will be able to
build big empires.

**Huge** — 2000 light years. Really really big.

Planet Density
--------------

Normal planet density in |original| is approximately two planets for every
10,000 ly². That is, if you divided the universe up into squares 100 ly on each
side, there would on average be two planets in each square.

- **Sparse:** ~1.5 planets per 10,000 ly²
- **Normal:** ~2.0 planets per 10,000 ly²
- **Dense:** ~2.5 planets per 10,000 ly²
- **Packed:** ~3.75 planets per 10,000 ly²

.. note::

   The combinations Large/Packed, Huge/Dense, and Huge/Packed have reduced
   density (most noticeable for Huge/Packed), likely because of engine limits
   on the number of stars allowed.

Player Positions
----------------

Specifies how close players start relative to each other. Results are affected
by the number of players, universe size, and density.

Distant player positions is considered the option that gives the most fair (or
rather least unfair) placement.

Game Options
------------

**Beginner: Maximum Minerals**
   The starting concentration of all minerals on every planet is 100. For
   beginning players who don't want to deal with mineral shortages. Not
   recommended for advanced players.

**Slower Tech Advances**
   Research costs are doubled. Extends the game; makes long-term strategy a
   more important element of play.

**Accelerated BBS Play**
   Players start with a population 4× larger than normal. All planets have
   20% more minerals. Planets with poor mineral densities are improved slightly.
   A rough approximation: ~5k pop for every 1% GR (e.g., 10% GR → ~75,000,
   20% GR → ~125,000).

**No Random Events**
   Disables all random occurrences: new mineral and artifact discoveries,
   comets, habitability shifts, traders.

**Computer Players Form Alliances**
   Computer players prefer attacking you over attacking each other. Makes the
   game significantly more difficult.

**Public Player Scores**
   All players' scores are displayed in the Score sheet (F10) after the first
   20 turns of the game.

**Galaxy Clumping**
   Clusters or clumps star systems, allowing for faster initial expansion.
